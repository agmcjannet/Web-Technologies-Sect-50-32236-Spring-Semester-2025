Quick Bio

Hi! I'm Allison (she/her), a fourth-year student/Senior in my last semester, double major in Art and Media Arts, from Helena, Montana. I'm a third generation Griz, love reading and anything crafty (especially felt ornaments at the moment), and am a die-hard Griz football fan.

I have experience in graphic design, Yearbook creation, social media management, and have a strong focus on creative problem solving and visual storytelling using Adobe Creative Suite. I love working with Illustrator and Photoshop to create visually appealing artworks that express my point of view.

I am passionate about community engagement and am volunteering with the University of Montana Advocates, the student ambassadors on campus, as well as working with them to run The Big Sky Experience, previously named New Student Orientation. I have also been recognized for my service, including honors like Phi Kappa Phi and Mortar Board inductions

Inspiration

Identity and Place
My artwork is often a journey through the themes of identity, tradition, and the landscapes that have shaped my life. Many pieces I've created in my college career reflect my deep connection to the University of Montana, my Montana roots, and the personal symbols that have informed my artistic voice. From the comfort of my pets to the rugged wilderness, I explore themes of belonging, memory, and growth through diverse mediums, including painting, printmaking, digital illustration, and animation.
Personal Symbols
Recurring motifs, such as cats, bears, and other Montana wildlife, serve as symbols of both personal history and the natural world. The bear, for example, represents my connection to both the University and my family, while cats evoke comfort and companionship. These elements merge to tell a story of home, community, and the transformative experiences I've had while at UM.
Artistic Growth
The works I've created, though varied in medium and style, share a common thread: they are all expressions of my evolving relationship with place, family, and the stories that shape my life. Together, they form a narrative of artistic growth, personal exploration, and the enduring beauty of the world around me. My personal experience, including the landscapes, animals, and memories I have inspire my work.

Video Games, Art & Growth

The interactive story video game, Florence, left a lasting impression on me because of how it resonated with me emotionally. As I played, I felt a connection to the main character, Florence, who was navigating the highs and lows of relationships, rediscovering herself, and personal growth. The simple interactive puzzle mechanics made me feel involved in the story, increasing the emotional weight of each moment. The color palette and music reflected Florence's emotional journey, shifting from light and joyful to sad during difficult moments. It was a game that made me reflect on the importance of self-love and personal passions.
One moment that stands out was the chapter “Let Go,” where Florence found fulfillment in her art instead of relying on a romantic relationship. This transformation felt really satisfying, and I realized it was a personal lesson in valuing oneself and one's passions. The way the game combined emotional storytelling with gameplay made each chapter feel meaningful, and the end left me feeling relieved and fulfilled. It made me think about how, in our world today, many people struggle with balancing relationships and personal growth. Florence highlighted the importance of taking time to rediscover what makes us happy.
Games like Florence can help us confront things like our emotional well-being, self-identity, and personal growth. By showing relatable stories through interactive and immersive experiences, games can create empathy and understanding. The game Florence's depiction of Florence's emotional journey showed how games could explore themes of self-love and personal growth in a way that might resonate with players. I believe this kind of story can help us see some of the emotional struggles people face, providing not just entertainment but also understanding.
